Welcome to dbMRNP

Mendelian randomization for risk factors of neuropsychiatric disorders DataBase

Examples:   Lung Neoplasms, Glioma, Protein-coding gene, miRNA, Radiosensitizer 

     Cancer is a complex disease and a leading cause of death, decreasing human life expectancy in worldwide. Radiotherapy (RT) is a primary treatment modality for cancer patients and is used for almost all solid tumors and palliative care. Therefore, it is crucial to investigate the radiation response mechanism that enhances tumor killing while reducing toxicity to surrounding normal tissues and cells, which could ensure better RT utilization and overcome the adverse side effects. Many researchers have focused on screening and mechanistic study of human tumor radiation response regulatory factors, but these resources are hidden in the vast amount of relevant literatures.
     Here we describe a comprehensive database dbCRAF with human cancer radiation response regulation factors derived from the literatures in PubMed. dbCRAF is an informative and functional resource of various factors related to the regulatory mechanisms of radiation response, including radiosensitivity, radioresistance and RT prognosis of cancer patients. Based on manual curation of 2,670 published articles, we extracted 4,448 associations for 30 types of human cancer. In general, dbCRAF offers a user-friendly web interface for data browsing, searching, analysis and visualization, with the aim to provide useful information and a one-stop service for researchers studying radiation response mechanism.
