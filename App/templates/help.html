1. Overview


Cancer is a complex disease that ranking the leading cause of death and decreasing human life expectancy in worldwide. Radiotherapy (RT) is a primary treatment modality for cancer patients and is used for almost all solid tumors and palliative care. Therefore, it is crucial to investigate the radiation response mechanism that enhances tumor killing while reducing toxicity to surrounding normal tissues and cells, which could ensure better RT utilization and overcome the adverse side effects. Many researchers have focused on screening and mechanistic study of human tumor radiation response regulatory factors, but these resources are hidden in the vast amount of relevant literatures.

Here we describe a comprehensive database dbCRAF with human cancer radiation response regulation factors derived from the literatures in PubMed. dbCRAF is an informative and functional resource of various factors related to the regulatory mechanisms of radiation response, including radiosensitivity, radioresistance and RT prognosis of cancer patients. Based on manual curation of 2,670 published articles, we extracted 4,448 associations for 30 types of human cancer. In general, dbCRAF offers a user-friendly web interface for data browsing, searching, analysis and visualization, with the aim to provide useful information and a one-stop service for researchers studying radiation response mechanism.

2. Web Browser Requirements


The dbCRAF requires a modern web browser with JavaScript and cookies enabled. To view the complex details, pop-ups must not be blocked. The following browsers have been thoroughly tested with dbCRAF:
• Mozilla Firefox, version 4 or above
• Internet Explorer, versions 9 or above
• Chrome, version 5 or above
• The latest version of Firefox and Chrome is recommended for visualization.

The dbCRAF database is freely available for the research community at here (http://dbCRAF.xialab.info/). Users are not required to register or login to access the data in the database.

5. Database usage


5.1 Data-Matrix

Matrix-like viewer for radiation response regulatory factor-cancer associations. The Data-Matrix page allows users to quickly search for related associations through "By All", "By Prediction", and "By Experiment". To view the related data for regulatory factor-cancer association of interest, users only need to click the link on the "count" number.



5.2 Search

Brief search results are presented as a table in the result page. Users can click the "Order" to view details about each entry in results from search.




5.3 Analysis


5.3.1 Cloud chart

This module allows users to visualize the most frequently studied radiation response regulatory factors in one or more cancer types based on regulatory factor-cancer associations.



5.3.2 Kaplan-Meier survival analysis

This module allows users to analyze the relationship between the expression levels of radiation response regulatory factor (miRNA, mRNA) and survival rate of cancer patients receiving RT derived from the Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) database.



5.3.3 KEGG pathway enrichment analysis

This module allows users to classify radiation response regulation genes and target genes of various cancer types according to KEGG pathway types, and to interpret the functions of these factors and display the analysis results form of a bubble chart.



5.3.4 Association network visualization

This module enables users to visualize the corresponding protein-protein interaction (PPI) network based on the radiation response regulatory factor-cancer and their target associations.
